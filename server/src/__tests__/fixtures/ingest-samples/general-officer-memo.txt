1. PURPOSE. This memorandum provides strategic guidance for military operations in the Western Pacific theater in support of the National Defense Strategy (NDS) and National Military Strategy (NMS).

2. STRATEGIC ENVIRONMENT. The People's Republic of China (PRC) continues to expand military capabilities in the South China Sea and East China Sea, threatening freedom of navigation and territorial integrity of allied nations. Recent developments include:
   a. Deployment of advanced surface-to-air missile systems on reclaimed islands
   b. Increased PLAN submarine activity in the Philippine Sea
   c. Aggressive cyber operations targeting allied C2 infrastructure
   d. Anti-satellite weapons testing threatening US space-based assets

3. STRATEGIC PRIORITIES.
   a. PRIORITY 1: Maintain freedom of navigation and overflight in international waters and airspace. All joint force components will prioritize operations ensuring unimpeded access to the South China Sea and East China Sea.
   b. PRIORITY 2: Protect and defend US and allied space-based capabilities. The increasing threat to GPS, SATCOM, and ISR satellites demands a robust space protection posture.
   c. PRIORITY 3: Deter adversary aggression through integrated deterrence. Joint force presence and readiness must demonstrate credible combat power across all domains.
   d. PRIORITY 4: Strengthen alliances and partnerships. Interoperability with Japan, Australia, Republic of Korea, and Philippines remains essential.
   e. PRIORITY 5: Deny adversary information advantage. Counter adversary ISR, cyber, and EW capabilities to maintain decision advantage.

4. COMMANDER'S INTENT. The Joint Force will maintain a posture that deters conflict while prepared to respond decisively if deterrence fails. All operations will be conducted in accordance with the law of armed conflict and standing rules of engagement.

5. ENDSTATE. A stable Western Pacific where freedom of navigation is assured, allied sovereignty is respected, and adversary coercion is effectively countered.

//SIGNED//
General James A. Morrison
